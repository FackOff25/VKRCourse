This work investigated a hybrid graph partitioning approach that combines the scalability of the Fennel streaming partitioning algorithm with the local optimization capabilities of the Kernighan--Lin refinement algorithm. The proposed method replaces the computationally expensive coarsening and uncoarsening stages of the METIS framework with online partitioning using Fennel, while periodically applying KL refinement to improve partition quality.

The experimental evaluation demonstrated that the proposed approach consistently reduced the graph cut compared to the partitioning produced by Fennel alone. Furthermore, the refinement process typically converged after only two iterations for each pair of partitions and never required more than three iterations in the evaluated experiments. These observations support the hypothesis that the partitioning generated by Fennel is of sufficiently high quality for efficient KL refinement, allowing the computational cost of refinement to remain low while improving partition quality.

Although the current evaluation was performed on synthetic graphs of moderate size, the results indicate that the proposed hybrid approach represents a practical compromise between partition quality and computational efficiency. It is therefore well suited for distributed graph databases and other systems that require scalable online graph partitioning while minimizing inter-partition communication.
